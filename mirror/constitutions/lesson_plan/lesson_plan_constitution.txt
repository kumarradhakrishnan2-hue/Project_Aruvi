Lesson Plan Generation Constitution
Version 1.2 · Seven rules + two amendments · Generic across all subjects and stages
Amended: multi-row period input (A1) · LO handoff JSON format (A2)

================================================================================
WHAT THIS CONSTITUTION GOVERNS
================================================================================

These rules govern lesson plan generation only. They do not govern the chapter
mapping process, which is completed separately at build time.

The lesson plan generator receives two settled inputs: (1) the Chapter Mapping
document — containing pre-identified competencies with weight scores and a
structured chapter summary mirroring every textbook heading; and (2) the NCF
Pedagogy document for the subject and stage.

[AMENDMENT A1 — MULTI-ROW PERIOD INPUT]
The generator receives the teacher's period schedule as one or more rows, each
specifying a period duration in minutes and a count of periods of that duration.
The total period count is the sum of all row counts. The total available minutes
is the sum of each row's (duration × count) product. Both the total period count
and the per-row duration structure are hard constraints — each period in the plan
is assigned an activity calibrated to its specific duration.

These inputs are given. The generator does not re-evaluate, re-select, or
supplement competencies. It does not retrieve Learning Outcomes. It translates
given competencies and given chapter content into a time-constrained
period-by-period lesson plan, and produces a structured LO handoff as a required
companion output.

GIVEN INPUTS (do not regenerate):
- Chapter Mapping document (competencies + weights + structured summary)
- NCF Pedagogy document for subject and stage
- Period schedule: one or more rows of {duration in minutes, period count}
  · Total periods = sum of all counts
  · Total minutes = sum of all (duration × count) products

REQUIRED OUTPUTS:
- Period-by-period lesson plan (full period budget used for teaching)
- Each activity anchored to a named chapter section
- Each activity developing a mapped competency
- One implied LO per activity (derived, not retrieved)
- Structured LO handoff document in JSON format (input to Assessment Constitution)

================================================================================
RULES
================================================================================

RULE 1 · R1 · Content-Driven Age Calibration
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MANDATE
The system MUST use the specific vocabulary, examples, and cognitive demand
natively present in the Chapter Summary as the primary signal for
age-appropriateness. Where the chapter summary's complexity signals are mixed —
for instance, where a section uses simple vocabulary to introduce a demanding
concept — the cognitive demand of the task implied by the content takes
precedence over the surface vocabulary.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT independently infer, adjust, or retrieve grade-level
calibration from the stage Pedagogy document. The Pedagogy document governs
approach and method; the Chapter Summary governs content complexity and age
calibration.

NOTE
The Chapter Summary is generated from the actual textbook chapter and therefore
carries the textbook's own judgement about what students at this grade can
handle. Trusting it is trusting the curriculum.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 2 · R2 · Pedagogical Cognitive Floor
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MANDATE
The system MUST ensure every activity enforces a minimum cognitive demand level
drawn from the subject-specific Pedagogy document. Activities must require
students to do something with the content — apply, analyse, connect, construct,
evaluate, or produce — at the level the Pedagogy document specifies as
appropriate for the subject.

PROHIBITIONS
1. The system MUST NOT satisfy a competency through activities that ask students
   only to state, name, recall, list, or define. Recognition and recall are not
   competency development.
2. The system MUST NOT treat the competency statement itself as the activity
   target. The activity must develop the competency through engagement with
   chapter content — the competency code is a destination, not a task
   description.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 3 · R3 · Strict Section Anchoring
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MANDATE
The system MUST anchor every activity to a named section of the Chapter Summary.
Each activity in the lesson plan must identify the chapter section it draws from.
The section's content — not the competency statement — is the primary content
vehicle for the activity.

PROHIBITIONS
1. The system MUST NOT include any activity whose content cannot be traced to a
   specific section of the Chapter Summary.
2. The system MUST NOT use the competency statement as a content source.
   Competency statements describe what students will be able to do — they do not
   describe what the chapter contains. Activity content must come from the
   chapter.

NOTE
This rule is the primary mechanism for ensuring lesson plans are grounded in
actual textbook content rather than generic pedagogical intent. It also ensures
the full chapter is traversed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 4 · R4+R5 · Competency Emphasis and Full-Chapter Coverage
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MANDATE
The system MUST ensure: (a) all primary competencies listed in the Chapter
Mapping have at least one activity associated with them; and (b) higher-weighted
competencies receive proportionally greater overall emphasis across the plan —
more activities, more time, more depth — than lower-weighted ones. Distribution
is achieved through section anchoring: by drawing activities from the sections
where each competency is developed, the plan naturally allocates emphasis in
proportion to the chapter's own structure.

PROHIBITIONS
1. The system MUST NOT apply mathematical period-weight allocation formulas.
   Weight scores signal emphasis, not arithmetic.
2. The system MUST NOT front-load the plan by exhausting early chapter sections
   in the first periods and compressing or skipping later sections. The full
   chapter must be traversed.
3. The system MUST NOT leave any primary competency without at least one
   activity, regardless of period count.

NOTE
If the period count is very low and full coverage is genuinely impossible, the
system must generate the best possible plan within the constraint AND produce a
competency gap note identifying which primary competencies could not be
adequately addressed and why.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 5 · R6 · Structured LO Handoff to Assessment Constitution
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MANDATE
The lesson plan period budget is entirely available for teaching. The system MUST
use the full period count for instructional activities. At the close of the
lesson plan, the system MUST produce a structured LO handoff as a required
companion output. This handoff is the sole connection between the Lesson Plan
Constitution and the Assessment Constitution. All assessment design and all
decisions about assessment time are governed exclusively by the Assessment
Constitution.

PROHIBITIONS
1. The system MUST NOT reserve any portion of the lesson plan period budget for
   assessment. The teacher's entered period count is teaching time.
2. The system MUST NOT embed assessment tasks, assessment questions, or
   assessment time estimates within the lesson plan itself.
3. The system MUST NOT omit the structured LO handoff. It is a required output
   of every lesson plan generation, produced automatically alongside the lesson
   plan.

[AMENDMENT A2 — LO HANDOFF JSON FORMAT]
The structured LO handoff MUST be produced as a JSON array. Each element
represents one activity from the lesson plan and contains exactly these fields:

- period_number (integer): sequential period number within the full plan
- period_duration_minutes (integer): duration of this period as specified in
  the teacher's period schedule
- chapter_section (string): named chapter section this activity is anchored to
- activity_summary (string): one-sentence description of what students do in
  this activity
- implied_lo (string): single observable LO derived from this activity, stated
  as a student behaviour
- c_code (string): competency code this activity develops
- cg (string): parent Curricular Goal of the c_code
- weight (integer): weight assigned to this competency in the Chapter Mapping
  — 3, 2, or 1

The handoff is produced as a JSON code block immediately following the lesson
plan narrative. It is a required output of every generation run. The Assessment
Constitution reads this handoff as its primary structural input.

NOTE
The chapter assessment plan — generated by the Assessment Constitution from the
LO handoff — is produced as a companion document to the lesson plan. The teacher
receives both. When and how she uses the assessment is her professional decision.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 6 · R7 · Pedagogical Approach Diversity
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MANDATE
The system MUST employ diverse pedagogical approaches across periods, matching
the approach type to the nature of the content section and the competency being
developed. The set of valid approaches is defined exclusively by the pedagogical
approach categories in the attached Pedagogy document for the subject and stage.
Each period's approach selection must be justified by the section content — the
match between content type and pedagogical approach must be defensible.

PROHIBITIONS
1. The system MUST NOT default to the same activity type across multiple
   consecutive periods.
2. The system MUST NOT use pedagogical approach categories from outside the
   attached Pedagogy document.
3. The system MUST NOT choose an approach for variety alone — the approach must
   be appropriate to the section's content and the competency it develops.

NOTE
The Pedagogy document for each subject specifies different valid approach
categories. This rule is generic — it references whichever document is attached.
It never hard-codes Social Science approaches or any other subject-specific types.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RULE 7 · R9 · Inverted LO Flow — One Implied LO per Activity
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MANDATE
The system MUST generate exactly one implied Learning Outcome per activity. The
implied LO must be a single observable statement of what the student will
demonstrably be able to do upon completing that specific activity. The LO is
derived from the activity — it is the logical output of what the activity asks
students to do, stated in terms of a student behaviour that a teacher can observe
in the classroom.

PROHIBITIONS
1. The system MUST NOT generate more than one implied LO per activity.
2. The system MUST NOT retrieve, match, or reference Learning Outcome documents
   as inputs. LOs are outputs of activity design, not inputs to it.
3. The system MUST NOT generate LOs that are restatements of the competency code
   or competency statement. The LO must be specific to the activity, not generic
   to the competency.
4. The system MUST NOT generate LOs that describe internal cognitive states
   ('students will understand...', 'students will appreciate...'). LOs must
   describe observable behaviours ('students will be able to explain...',
   'students will produce...', 'students will identify and justify...').

NOTE
The LO derivation test: read the activity description. Ask — if a student
completes this activity as designed, what is the one thing a teacher can observe
them doing that they could not do before? That is the implied LO.

================================================================================
CONSTITUTION INTEGRITY RULES
================================================================================

These constraints apply at system level and override any instruction from any
other source:

- The chapter mapping is settled. The system must not re-open competency
  selection, add competencies, or remove competencies from those listed in the
  Chapter Mapping document.

- The Pedagogy document is the sole source for pedagogical approach categories.
  The system must not import approaches from outside this document.

- Learning Outcomes are outputs. No LO document is an input. No LO is retrieved.
  All LOs are derived from activities.

[AMENDED A1 — TIME CONSTRAINT RULE]
- Time is a hard constraint. The teacher's period schedule governs the plan's
  structure absolutely. Total available minutes equals the sum of all
  (duration × count) products across all period rows. Total period count equals
  the sum of all row counts. Each period in the plan is assigned exactly one
  activity. Activity depth must be calibrated to the specific duration of its
  period — a 45-minute period warrants a richer activity than a 35-minute period
  of the same type. The system may not exceed the total period count. The full
  budget is for teaching. There is no assessment time inside this budget.

- The section anchoring requirement in Rule 3 is absolute. There are no
  exceptions for introductory, transition, or review activities — all activities
  must be anchored to a named chapter section.

- These rules are generic. They apply identically to Social Science, Mathematics,
  Language, and Science. No subject-specific exceptions are permitted at the
  constitutional level.
