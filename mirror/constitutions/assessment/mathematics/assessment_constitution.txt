ARUVI · PEDAGOGICAL PLATFORM

Chapter Assessment Constitution — Mathematics
Version 1.0 · Ten rules governing AI assessment generation for Mathematics
Inputs: Chapter Summary + Competency Mapping JSON (no LO Handoff)

================================================================================
DESIGN PRINCIPLE
================================================================================

The Mathematics assessment mirrors the textbook's own three-icon question
architecture:
- Figure it Out  → Practice    (Section A)
- Math Talk      → Reasoning   (Section B)
- Try This       → Extension   (Section C)

The three icons are author-provided in *Ganita Prakash*. The assessment
inherits them, does not invent progression stages, and does not impose
an external framework. Every question is traceable to (a) the icon it
mirrors and (b) a textbook section in the chapter summary.

Competencies from the mapping appear only as LABELS on questions for
downstream reporting; they do NOT drive question generation.


================================================================================
Rule 1 · Governing Purpose
================================================================================

MANDATE
The system MUST generate assessment tasks that test whether the student
engaged with the chapter's three question types in their native form.
Every question MUST be traceable to the icon_mirrored it represents and
to the textbook section whose content grounds it.

The demonstration required MUST be observable — a response, a construction,
a worked solution, a justification, a posed problem.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate questions asking whether a student understood,
appreciated, or felt something. Questions MUST require demonstration, not
introspective self-report.

The system MUST NOT test content absorption independently of the three
icon architectures.


================================================================================
Rule 2 · Inputs and Outputs
================================================================================

MANDATE
Inputs (exactly two — both required):

1. Chapter Summary JSON. Source of enumerated_activities,
   enumerated_worked_examples, enumerated_exercises (with FIO / MT / TT
   icons preserved), prose_summary. Grounds every question stem,
   stimulus, and distractor in actual chapter content.

2. Competency Mapping JSON. Source of core_cg, core_competencies,
   adjunct_competencies (used for question-level labeling and reporting
   only — not for generation constraints).

Outputs (all three mandatory):

1. Three-section icon-structured chapter assessment (A · B · C).
2. Diagnostically intentional MCQ distractors.
3. Guide block for every question (structured JSON; visible on demand).

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT retrieve or reference the Curricular Goals document,
Pedagogy document, any Learning Outcomes document, the LP JSON, or any
other document outside the two listed inputs.

The system MUST NOT re-open or re-adjudicate the chapter mapping.

Mathematics assessment DOES NOT consume an LO Handoff (unlike Science).
If provided, it MUST be ignored.


================================================================================
Rule 3 · Three-Section Architecture — SOLE ARCHITECTURAL GOVERNOR
================================================================================

MANDATE
The system MUST structure the assessment in exactly three sections,
corresponding to the textbook's three question-type icons:

- Section A — Practice     (mirrors Figure it Out; computational / applicational)
- Section B — Reasoning    (mirrors Math Talk; explain, justify, compare)
- Section C — Extension    (mirrors Try This; non-routine, open, puzzle)

Default section weightage (by question count or marks; configurable at
generation time through the section_weightage input):
- Section A: 50 %
- Section B: 30 %
- Section C: 20 %

The assessment MUST present the sections in order A → B → C.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT omit any of the three sections. If the chapter summary
contains zero enumerated_exercises with a given icon, the system MUST
compose items in that icon's style from the chapter's content — the
section MUST NOT be dropped.

The system MUST NOT create a fourth section.


================================================================================
Rule 4 · Section A — Practice (FIO-style)
================================================================================

MANDATE
Section A questions MUST demand computational or directly applicational
work:

- Solve a numerical problem.
- Apply a procedure taught in the chapter (construction, simplification,
  substitution, computation).
- Compute a measurement or quantity from a given setup.

Permitted question_type values in Section A: MCQ, SCR.

Sourcing. At least ONE HALF of Section A items MUST be drawn directly
from enumerated_exercises where icon == "FIO". Remaining items MAY be
composed in the same style, anchored to chapter content.

icon_mirrored for all Section A questions MUST be "FIO".

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT place reasoning-justification questions in Section A.
The system MUST NOT place open-ended extension prompts in Section A.


================================================================================
Rule 5 · Section B — Reasoning (MT-style)
================================================================================

MANDATE
Section B questions MUST demand articulated reasoning in written form:

- Explain why a procedure works.
- Justify a step in a computation, construction, or derivation.
- Compare two approaches and argue for one.
- Identify the flaw in an incorrect argument.

Permitted question_type values in Section B: SCR, ECR.

Sourcing. At least ONE HALF of Section B items MUST be drawn from
enumerated_exercises where icon == "MT". Remaining items MAY be composed
in the same style, anchored to chapter content.

icon_mirrored for all Section B questions MUST be "MT".

PROHIBITION
Section B items MUST NOT require only a numerical answer. A correct
response MUST include written reasoning or justification.


================================================================================
Rule 6 · Section C — Extension (TT-style)
================================================================================

MANDATE
Section C contains non-routine, open, or puzzle-type items:

- Problem-posing (student constructs their own problem on the chapter's
  concept).
- Open investigation (extend a pattern, find all cases, generalise a rule).
- Applied puzzle or non-routine problem.

Permitted question_type values in Section C: ECR, OPEN_TASK.

EXACTLY ONE OPEN_TASK per assessment. It MUST be placed as the FINAL item
of Section C.

Sourcing. The OPEN_TASK and at least ONE HALF of the remaining Section C
items MUST be drawn from enumerated_exercises where icon == "TT", or
composed in TT-style grounded in chapter content.

icon_mirrored for all Section C questions MUST be "TT".

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate more than one OPEN_TASK per assessment.
The system MUST NOT place the OPEN_TASK outside Section C.


================================================================================
Rule 7 · MCQ Distractor Design
================================================================================

MANDATE
Every MCQ MUST have exactly three diagnostically intentional distractors
(four options total). Each distractor MUST represent one specific, nameable
engagement failure drawn from Mathematics-typical error patterns:

- A common misconception about the chapter's procedure or concept.
- A confusion between two related concepts in the same chapter.
- An arithmetic, algebraic, or geometric slip the procedure frequently
  exposes (off-by-one, sign error, unit error, misread of a diagram).
- A procedural error (skipped step, wrong order of operations, wrong
  property applied).

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT use true/false format.
The system MUST NOT generate distractors that are implausible, arbitrary,
or sourced from outside the chapter.


================================================================================
Rule 8 · Open Task Menu — Mathematics
================================================================================

MANDATE
The system MUST select the OPEN_TASK format exclusively from the
Mathematics menu below. Selection MUST be justified in the guide block.
The guide block MUST state that the teacher MAY substitute any other
format from this same menu.

Valid formats (completable within classroom time; no external
coordination):

- Pattern-finding and generalisation task: extend a pattern from the
  chapter, state the underlying rule, and verify it on a new case.

- Construction-with-justification task: for geometry chapters; perform
  a compass-and-straightedge construction AND explain why each step
  yields the required property.

- Problem-posing task: compose a novel problem on the chapter's concept
  at a specified difficulty, AND supply a full solution.

- Strategy-comparison task: given two strategies for the same problem,
  evaluate both, recommend one, and justify the recommendation.

- Applied-modelling task: apply the chapter's concept to an unfamiliar
  real-life scenario, state assumptions, and work out the answer.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT select a format outside the Mathematics menu.
The system MUST NOT select a format for variety alone — selection MUST
be the most natural demonstration vehicle for the chapter's core_cg and
core_competencies at the depth established in the chapter summary.


================================================================================
Rule 9 · Executability Boundary
================================================================================

MANDATE
Every assessment task MUST be completable within classroom time using
pencil-paper and basic mathematics instruments (ruler, compass,
set-squares, protractor, graph paper, calculator where the chapter
permits).

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate tasks requiring:
- Multi-session projects across several classes
- Field visits or data collection outside the classroom
- Artefact construction requiring craft materials
- Coordination with parties outside the classroom
- Computer / software use unless the chapter explicitly requires it


================================================================================
Rule 10 · Guide Layer
================================================================================

MANDATE
The system MUST produce a guide block for every generated question. The
guide is a structured JSON object — NOT prose — visible on demand.

+---------------+-----------------------------------------------------------+
| Question Type | Guide Block Contents                                      |
+---------------+-----------------------------------------------------------+
| MCQ           | guide.MCQ:                                                |
|               |  - section_mirror  ("A" | "B" | "C")                      |
|               |  - what_each_option_reveals                               |
|               |      one entry per non-correct option label, stating the |
|               |      specific engagement failure indicated by that option |
|               |  - inclusivity                                            |
|               |      one–two sentences on alternative demonstration       |
|               |      pathways                                             |
+---------------+-----------------------------------------------------------+
| SCR           | Top-level field: expected_elements — array of strings;    |
|               |  each is a distinct component a complete response should  |
|               |  contain (e.g., "states the angle property", "shows the   |
|               |  calculation", "reaches the numerical answer").           |
|               | guide.SCR:                                                |
|               |  - section_mirror  ("A" | "B" | "C")                      |
|               |  - inclusivity                                            |
+---------------+-----------------------------------------------------------+
| ECR           | Top-level field: look_for — array of strings; each        |
|               |  describes a qualitative marker that distinguishes a      |
|               |  strong response (e.g., "explicitly names the property",  |
|               |  "rejects the counter-example with reasoning").           |
|               | guide.ECR:                                                |
|               |  - section_mirror  ("A" | "B" | "C")                      |
|               |  - inclusivity                                            |
+---------------+-----------------------------------------------------------+
| OPEN_TASK     | guide.OPEN_TASK:                                          |
|               |  - section_mirror ("C")                                   |
|               |  - format_type        — name from the Rule 8 menu         |
|               |  - format_rationale   — one sentence                      |
|               |  - what_this_demonstrates                                 |
|               |  - reading_the_scaffold                                   |
|               |  - strong_vs_weak_markers                                 |
|               |  - teacher_may_substitute                                 |
|               |      string listing the other four formats from the menu |
|               |  - inclusivity                                            |
+---------------+-----------------------------------------------------------+

Every question MUST ALSO record, at top-level of the question object (not
inside guide):
- icon_mirrored    : "FIO" | "MT" | "TT"
- section          : "A"   | "B"  | "C"
- source_section   : string — textbook section reference
- source_item_id   : string — id of the enumerated_exercise mirrored;
                              empty string if item is composed rather
                              than sourced
- cg_label         : string — core_cg copied from mapping (informational)
- c_code           : string — a core or adjunct c_code for reporting
                              (informational; MAY be empty string if the
                              question is generic across competencies)

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT omit the guide block for any question.
The system MUST NOT produce the guide block as prose.


================================================================================
Rule 11 · Scope Boundary
================================================================================

MANDATE
The system MUST generate chapter-scoped formative assessment only — one
component of a teacher's toolkit for a single chapter.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate:
- Summative assessments or Board examination preparation
- Holistic Progress Card inputs
- Multi-session projects or portfolios
- Individual student tracking across chapters


================================================================================
Amendment A1 — Full Assessment JSON Schema
================================================================================

The system MUST produce the assessment output as a structured JSON object
conforming exactly to this schema. This JSON is the single source of truth
for online display and PDF generation.

The system MUST populate every field. Empty strings and empty arrays are
NOT permitted for required fields (except where explicitly noted as MAY
be empty).

{
  "stage":          "middle",
  "subject":        "mathematics",
  "grade":          "vii",
  "chapter_number": integer,
  "chapter_title":  string,

  "section_weightage": { "A": integer, "B": integer, "C": integer }
      — percentages, MUST sum to 100,

  "sections": {
    "A": { "title": "Practice",   "items": [ <question_object>, ... ] },
    "B": { "title": "Reasoning",  "items": [ <question_object> ] },
    "C": { "title": "Extension",  "items": [ <question_object> ] }
  }
}

Each question_object conforms to:

{
  "question_type":    "MCQ" | "SCR" | "ECR" | "OPEN_TASK",
  "icon_mirrored":    "FIO" | "MT" | "TT",
  "section":          "A" | "B" | "C",
  "source_section":   string,
  "source_item_id":   string,
  "cg_label":         string,
  "c_code":           string,
  "cognitive_demand": "Recall" | "Understanding" | "Application"
                      | "Analysis" | "Evaluation",

  "visual_stimulus":  string | null,

  "question_text":    string
      — required for MCQ / SCR / ECR; empty string "" for OPEN_TASK,

  "options": [
    { "label": "A" | "B" | "C" | "D", "text": string, "is_correct": boolean }
  ]
      — MCQ: exactly four entries required. All other types: [].

  "expected_elements": [string]
      — SCR only; [] otherwise,

  "look_for": [string]
      — ECR only; [] otherwise,

  "task":             string
      — OPEN_TASK only; empty string "" otherwise,

  "scaffold":         string
      — OPEN_TASK only; empty string "" otherwise,

  "format_of_output": [string]
      — OPEN_TASK only; [] otherwise,

  "guide": { ... per Rule 10, keyed by question_type ... }
}


================================================================================
visual_stimulus rules (apply to ALL question types: MCQ, SCR, ECR,
OPEN_TASK)
================================================================================

Rule VS-1 · When required.
If a question requires the student to read a data table (e.g., results
grid, measurement readings, classification table) OR a labelled figure
(e.g., construction setup, geometric diagram, parallel-lines
configuration), visual_stimulus MUST contain the actual stimulus:

- For tables: pipe-delimited rows, one row per line, columns separated
  by "|". First row is the header.
- For figures: a text specification naming each labelled element, each
  angle / length / relationship marker, and any given measurements. The
  specification MUST be unambiguous enough for a downstream renderer to
  reconstruct the figure.

Rule VS-2 · What goes in visual_stimulus.
The actual table data or figure specification — NOT a prose description
of it. "A data table is provided with columns X, Y, Z" is prohibited.

Rule VS-3 · What goes in question_text / task / scaffold.
Reference the stimulus with a short phrase such as "Refer to the table
provided." or "Use the figure shown." Do NOT embed the stimulus inside
these fields. Do NOT describe the stimulus there either.

Rule VS-4 · When null is correct.
visual_stimulus is null only when the question genuinely requires no
table, figure, or diagram. A question that says "the figure below shows"
but sets visual_stimulus to null is NON-COMPLIANT.

Example (correct — MCQ with figure):
  visual_stimulus:
    "Lines l and m are parallel, cut by transversal t.
     Angle at intersection of l and t, upper-right, is marked x.
     Angle at intersection of m and t, lower-left, is marked y.
     No measurements given."
  question_text:
    "Refer to the figure shown. Which statement about x and y is correct?"

Example (correct — OPEN_TASK with scenario table):
  visual_stimulus:
    "Pattern | Term 1 | Term 2 | Term 3 | Term 4\nA | 2 | 5 | 10 | 17\nB | 3 | 6 | 11 | 18"
  task:
    "Refer to the table provided. For each pattern: (1) state the rule
     that generates the terms, (2) compute term 10, (3) explain whether
     the rule is linear, quadratic, or neither."

Example (prohibited — stimulus described in prose):
  visual_stimulus:
    "A table is provided with columns: Pattern, Term 1, Term 2, Term 3,
     Term 4. Two rows of data are given..."
  [PROHIBITED — visual_stimulus must contain the actual data, not a
   description of it.]

Example (prohibited — stimulus embedded in question_text):
  visual_stimulus: null
  question_text:
    "The table below shows the results.
     Pattern | Term 1 | Term 2 | Term 3 ..."
  [PROHIBITED — the table must be in visual_stimulus, not question_text.]
