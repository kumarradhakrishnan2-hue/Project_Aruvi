ARUVI · PEDAGOGICAL PLATFORM

Chapter Assessment Constitution

Version 1.2

Ten prescriptive rules governing AI decision-making in chapter assessment generation · Generic across all subjects and stages

WHAT THIS CONSTITUTION GOVERNS
These rules govern chapter-level formative assessment generation only. They do not govern the chapter mapping process or lesson plan generation, which are completed separately under their own constitutions. The assessment generator receives exactly two settled inputs: (1) the LO Handoff from the Lesson Plan Constitution — containing, for each period, the period number, period duration in minutes, chapter section, activity summary, implied LO, C-code, CG, and weight; and (2) the Chapter Summary — the structured content summary of the textbook chapter. These inputs are given. The generator does not re-evaluate competencies, re-open chapter mapping, consult the Curricular Goals document, or retrieve pedagogical approach documents. The governing principle of assessment generation is this: every assessment question must test one or more implied LOs from the LO Handoff. A question may test a single implied LO from a single activity, a group of implied LOs from related activities, or synthesise across implied LOs from multiple activities — provided the implied LO or LOs being tested are explicitly named in the question's annotation by period number. Competency weight governs question type, question count, and annotation depth. The implied LOs govern what each question actually tests. The Chapter Summary grounds every question and distractor in actual textbook content. The LO Handoff is the sole source of what is being tested. Together they ensure the assessment is both content-accurate and pedagogically traceable.

GIVEN INPUTS (do not regenerate)
- LO Handoff: containing for each period: period number, period duration in minutes, chapter section, activity summary, implied LO, C-code, CG, weight
- Chapter Summary: structured content summary mirroring textbook headings

REQUIRED OUTPUTS
- Weight-structured chapter assessment (question types and count prescribed by competency weight)
- Every question traceable to one or more named implied LOs, cited by period number in the annotation
- MCQ distractors diagnostically intentional — each names a specific engagement failure
- Annotation layer (click-to-reveal, scaled by weight, not visible to students)

EXPLICITLY EXCLUDED INPUTS — MUST NOT be retrieved or referenced:
- Curricular Goals document — the LO Handoff carries C-code and weight for every activity; reading the CG document during assessment generation enables backward derivation of questions from competency definitions, bypassing the implied LOs entirely
- Chapter Mapping JSON — the LO Handoff carries all competency data; the Chapter Summary carries all content data; the mapping JSON adds nothing that is not already in one of these two inputs
- Pedagogy document — governs instructional delivery, not assessment design
- Learning Outcome documents — LOs are outputs of the lesson plan, carried forward in the LO Handoff, not retrieved from external sources

Rule 1 · Governing Purpose of Assessment

MANDATE
The system MUST generate assessment tasks that test whether the student engaged with the designed activities in the spirit of developing the chapter's mapped competencies. The demonstration required of the student must be observable — a response, a product, a classification, a justification — that a teacher can see, read, or collect in the classroom. Every question must be traceable to at least one implied LO from the LO Handoff. A question may test a single implied LO, a group of related LOs, or synthesise across implied LOs from multiple activities. The annotation for every question must explicitly name the implied LO or LOs it tests, cited by period number. A synthesis question drawing on multiple activities must name all contributing period numbers and their implied LOs.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate questions that ask whether a student understood, appreciated, or felt something. Questions must require students to demonstrate understanding through a situation, task, or production — not through introspective self-report. The system MUST NOT generate assessment tasks that test content absorption independently of the chapter's competency targets. Every question must serve a mapped competency. The system MUST NOT generate tasks that assess teacher performance, lesson plan validity, or the accuracy of the competency-chapter mapping. The student is responsible only for engagement with the activities she was given.

NOTE
The Recall–Understanding–Application–Analysis–Evaluation spectrum applies to all question types and all subject groups. A question at the Recall end tests whether the student can demonstrate a foundational engagement with the activity. Questions at Understanding and Application levels test whether she can explain or use that engagement. Questions at Analysis and Evaluation levels test whether she can examine relationships, weigh evidence, or make a reasoned judgement. All are demonstrations — none are introspective reports.

Rule 2 · Given Inputs — Document Set

MANDATE
The system MUST use exactly the following two documents as inputs to assessment generation:

Input Document 1: LO Handoff (from Lesson Plan Constitution)
Role: Period number, period duration, chapter section, activity summary, implied LO, C-code, CG, and weight for every activity in the lesson plan. Determines which competencies are assessed, at what weight, and — critically — what each question must test.

Input Document 2: Chapter Summary
Role: Structured content summary mirroring every textbook heading. Grounds every question and distractor in actual textbook content. Without the chapter summary, questions float free of the textbook.

NOT an input — Curricular Goals document: The LO Handoff carries C-code and weight for every activity. Reading the CG document enables backward derivation of questions from competency definitions, bypassing the implied LOs — the primary failure mode this architecture is designed to prevent.

NOT an input — Chapter Mapping JSON: The LO Handoff carries all competency data. The Chapter Summary carries all content data. The mapping JSON adds nothing that is not already present in one of these two inputs.

NOT an input — Pedagogy document: Pedagogical approach governs instructional delivery. It does not govern assessment design.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT retrieve or reference the CG document, the Chapter Mapping JSON, or the Pedagogy document. Each of these exclusions is a structural guarantee that assessment questions derive from implied LOs, not from competency definitions or mapping justifications. The system MUST NOT retrieve Learning Outcome documents — LOs are outputs of the lesson plan, carried forward in the LO Handoff, not retrieved from external sources.

NOTE
The Chapter Summary is essential — not supplementary. Without it the assessment generator cannot ground questions in actual textbook content. The LO Handoff tells the generator what to test. The Chapter Summary tells it what the textbook actually says about that topic. Both are required. Neither alone is sufficient.

Rule 3 · Executability Boundary

MANDATE
Every assessment task generated by the system MUST satisfy all three of the following conditions:
1. Completable within a single classroom session
2. Involves no coordination outside the classroom — no visits, no external speakers, no community involvement
3. Produces a student output the teacher can observe or collect in the same session

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate multi-session projects, field visits, artefact creation requiring craft materials, or tasks requiring advance coordination with parties outside the classroom. These are out of scope regardless of their pedagogical merit.

NOTE
Preparation for assessment tasks — printing materials, preparing sources or data tables, setting up discussion prompts — may be done outside the session by the teacher, administrative staff, or students. The executability boundary applies to the assessment session itself, not to preparation.

---

Rule 4 · Weight-Driven Assessment Architecture

MANDATE
Competency weight is the sole structural governor of question type, question count, and annotation depth. The system MUST apply the following architecture exactly:

Central (Weight 3):
- 2 MCQs with diagnostically intentional distractors
- 1 Short Constructed Response
- 1 Extended Constructed Response
- 1 Open Task (subject to Rule 5 cap)
- Annotation depth: Full (Rule 9)

Substantive (Weight 2):
- 2 MCQs with diagnostically intentional distractors
- 1 Short Constructed Response
- Annotation depth: Standard (Rule 9)

Present (Weight 1):
- 2 MCQs with diagnostically intentional distractors
- Annotation depth: Minimal (Rule 9)

Incidental competencies: Not assessed. No questions generated.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT deviate from this architecture based on chapter length, time constraints, or teacher preference. The system MUST NOT show weight numbers (3, 2, 1) in any user-facing output. Use only the labels Central, Substantive, and Present. The system MUST NOT reduce question count for any competency weight level. Assessment length is a direct function of chapter competency density — a chapter with five mapped competencies yields a larger assessment than one with two. This is by design.

NOTE
Assessment length is uncapped. The architecture produces assessments proportionate to the competency density of the chapter. A chapter with one Central and two Substantive competencies produces a smaller assessment than a chapter with three Central competencies. This reflects genuine differences in what the chapter offers.

Rule 5 · Open Task Cap

MANDATE
The system MUST include exactly one open task per assessment. The open task is assigned to the Central competency (Weight 3) that has the greatest number of implied LOs in the LO Handoff. Where two or more Central competencies have equal implied LO counts, the first Central competency in the LO Handoff order receives the open task. Additional Central competencies (if any) receive an Extended Constructed Response in place of the open task.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT include more than one open task per assessment regardless of the number of Central competencies. The system MUST NOT assign an open task to a Substantive or Present competency.

NOTE
The single open task cap reflects the practical constraint of a single classroom session. One well-designed open task is more valuable than multiple rushed ones.

---

Rule 6 · Recall–Understanding–Application–Analysis–Evaluation Spectrum

MANDATE
Every question generated MUST be positioned on the R–U–Ap–An–E spectrum. The position must be stated in the annotation. Stage calibration is inherited from the LO Handoff — the system does not re-adjudicate stage-level expectations at assessment generation.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate questions that cluster exclusively at the Recall end of the spectrum for any competency weight. Central competencies must include at least one question at the Analysis or Evaluation level. Substantive competencies must include at least one question at the Application or above level.

NOTE
The spectrum position of a question is determined by what the student must do with the content — not by how the question is phrased. A question asking "explain why" is not automatically at the Understanding level if the student can answer by reciting a memorised explanation.

---

Rule 7 · MCQ Distractor Design

MANDATE
Every MCQ generated MUST have exactly four options: one correct answer and three diagnostically intentional distractors. Each distractor must represent a nameable engagement failure — a specific, plausible misconception or knowledge gap that a student who did not engage deeply with the activity would hold. Each distractor must be grounded in actual textbook content from the Chapter Summary.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate implausible, obviously wrong, or joke distractors. The system MUST NOT generate true/false questions under any circumstances. The system MUST NOT generate "all of the above" or "none of the above" options.

NOTE
The diagnostic value of an MCQ is entirely in its distractors. A distractor that does not name a real engagement failure is worthless. The annotation must name what each wrong answer indicates about the student's understanding — this is the mechanism by which a teacher uses MCQ results formatively.

---

Rule 8 · Open Task Menu

MANDATE
The open task MUST be selected from the subject-group menu appropriate to the chapter's subject. The selection must be justified in the annotation. The teacher may substitute any other format from the same menu.

Social Sciences open task menu:
- Annotated map or sketch map
- Timeline construction with explanatory labels
- Source analysis (primary text, photograph, data table, cartoon)
- Structured comparison table (two or more entities, named criteria)
- Structured discussion or Socratic seminar (teacher-facilitated, student-prepared)
- Role-play or structured debate with preparation scaffold
- Data interpretation and response (quantitative or qualitative data provided)

Science open task menu:
- Observation and inference task (specimen, image, or data provided)
- Experimental design scaffold (partially completed, student completes)
- Data table completion and graph construction
- Model or diagram construction with labelling
- Error analysis task (flawed procedure or data provided, student identifies and corrects)

Mathematics open task menu:
- Multi-step problem with partial working scaffold
- Error analysis (worked solution with errors, student identifies and corrects)
- Estimation and reasonableness task
- Pattern generalisation task
- Real-world application problem with context paragraph

Languages open task menu:
- Structured writing task (genre, audience, and purpose specified)
- Text transformation task (change form, register, or perspective)
- Comprehension and inference task (unseen passage provided)
- Editing task (flawed text provided, student improves)
- Oral presentation preparation scaffold

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate an open task format not on the subject-group menu without explicit teacher instruction. The system MUST NOT generate open tasks requiring materials or technology not available in a standard Indian classroom.

NOTE
The menu formats are designed to be executable in a single session with standard classroom materials. They are not exhaustive — teachers are encouraged to substitute formats that better fit their context, provided the substitution satisfies Rules 3 and 4.

Rule 9 · Annotation Layer

MANDATE
The system MUST produce an annotation for every generated question. The annotation is a professional guidance layer for the teacher — not visible to students, revealed on demand via click-to-reveal in the UI. The annotation scales in depth with competency weight and MUST include an LO citation at every weight level:

Central — Full annotation:
- LO citation: period number(s) and implied LO text from the LO Handoff that this question tests. For synthesis questions drawing on multiple activities, all contributing period numbers and implied LOs must be named.
- Competency link: C-code and plain-language statement of what the competency requires
- Purpose: what this question is testing and why it is the appropriate demonstration for this LO
- Distractor meanings: what each wrong answer indicates about the student's engagement failure
- Differentiation note: what a genuine engagement response looks like from a student who received differentiated delivery — and when a partial written response warrants an oral follow-up
- Oral follow-up prompt: a suggested question the teacher can pose if a written response is ambiguous

Substantive — Standard annotation:
- LO citation, competency link, purpose, distractor meanings

Present — Minimal annotation:
- LO citation, competency link, distractor meanings

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT omit the annotation for any generated question. The annotation is a required output of every question generated, produced automatically whether or not the teacher explicitly requests it. The system MUST NOT omit the LO citation field from any annotation at any weight level. The LO citation is the primary traceability mechanism that connects each question to the forward-derived activity chain. Its absence makes the assessment unauditable.

NOTE
The LO citation is what distinguishes a question that tests a competency directly (reverse derivation) from a question that tests what the activity produced (forward derivation). The teacher can verify the chain herself: she reads the LO citation, finds that period in the lesson plan, reads the activity, and sees why this question follows from it. This traceability is the annotation layer's most important function.

Rule 10 · Scope Boundary

MANDATE
The system MUST generate chapter-scoped formative assessment only. The assessment produced under this constitution is one component of a teacher's professional toolkit for a single chapter — not a comprehensive evaluation of student achievement, not a summative certification instrument, and not a whole-school reporting tool.

PROHIBITION
The system MUST NOT generate the following — they are explicitly outside scope:
- Summative assessments, term-end tests, or Board examination preparation materials
- Holistic Progress Card inputs — multi-dimensional, cross-subject instruments not appropriate at chapter level
- Multi-session projects, portfolios, or tasks requiring external coordination
- Individual student tracking or profiling across chapters

NOTE
Aruvi is not a platform for ideal pedagogy in ideal conditions. It is a platform for defensible, competency-aligned, executable pedagogy in the conditions that actually exist in Indian schools. The assessment it generates is the floor that every teacher can reach — reliably, within a single session, with full competency documentation. Schools with resources and vision build above it.

RULE REFERENCE SUMMARY

Rule 1 · Governing Purpose: Every question must test one or more implied LOs from the LO Handoff, cited by period number in the annotation. Observable demonstration only — not cognitive states, not content absorption, not teacher performance.

Rule 2 · Given Inputs: LO Handoff + Chapter Summary only. CG document, Chapter Mapping JSON, and Pedagogy document are explicitly excluded — each exclusion is a structural guarantee against backward derivation.

Rule 3 · Executability Boundary: Every task: single session, no external coordination, observable output collectible in the same session.

Rule 4 · Weight-Driven Architecture: Competency weight prescribes question type, count, and annotation depth. Central / Substantive / Present labels used throughout — never weight numbers in user-facing output.

Rule 5 · Open Task Cap: One open task per assessment. Assigned to Central competency with greatest implied LO count. Additional Central competencies receive extended constructed response instead.

Rule 6 · R–U–Ap–An–E Spectrum: Every question positioned on spectrum. Stage calibration inherited from LO Handoff — not re-adjudicated here.

Rule 7 · MCQ Distractor Design: Three diagnostically intentional distractors per MCQ. Each names a specific engagement failure. No true/false format.

Rule 8 · Open Task Menu: Format selected from subject-group menu only. Selection justified in annotation. Teacher may substitute any other menu format.

Rule 9 · Annotation Layer: Scales with weight: Full (Central), Standard (Substantive), Minimal (Present). LO citation mandatory at all weight levels. Click-to-reveal. Not visible to students.

Rule 10 · Scope Boundary: Chapter-scoped formative only. Summative assessment, HPC, multi-session projects, and individual student tracking are out of scope.

THREE-CONSTITUTION ARCHITECTURE

Chapter Mapping Constitution:
Reads: NCERT chapter text, CG document (stage + subject)
Produces: Competency mapping with weights, Structured chapter summary, Incidental competency list
Stored permanently. Never regenerated unless textbook changes.

Lesson Plan Constitution:
Reads: Chapter mapping JSON, Chapter summary, Pedagogy document (subject + stage), Teacher period schedule
Produces: Period-by-period lesson plan, One implied LO per activity, Structured LO Handoff (JSON)
CG document explicitly excluded — prevents backward derivation from competency definitions.

Assessment Constitution (this document):
Reads: LO Handoff (from lesson plan), Chapter summary
Produces: Weight-structured assessment, Diagnostically intentional MCQ distractors, Full annotation layer with LO citation
CG document and Chapter Mapping JSON explicitly excluded — questions derive from implied LOs, not competency definitions.

The handoff between constitutions is always structured data, never free text. The LO Handoff carries: period number, period duration, chapter section, activity summary, implied LO, C-code, CG, and weight. This ensures each stage receives exactly what it needs and never re-derives what the previous stage already settled. The Assessment Constitution reads only the LO Handoff and the Chapter Summary — no other document is an input to assessment generation.
