================================================================================
Design principle
================================================================================

The textbook is the source of truth. The chapter summary is its faithful
mirror. The mapping procedure reads the summary and a single external
document — the Curricular Goals document — to identify which Curricular
Goal(s) and Competency(ies) the chapter is laid out to develop.

Mathematics chapters are typically laid out to develop one central CG, with
several process-oriented competencies (reasoning, creativity, computational
thinking, historical framing) running through them as adjuncts. The mapping
accordingly proceeds in THREE passes: (1) summary reading, (2) core CG
identification and core competency selection within that CG, (3) adjunct
competency scan across the remaining CGs.


================================================================================
Competency mapping — three-pass procedure
================================================================================

Rule 1 | Pass 1 — Chapter summary reading (CG-blind)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The system MUST read the chapter summary JSON in full before consulting the
CG document or any framework document. The system reads:

- prose_summary (section by section)
- enumerated_activities
- enumerated_worked_examples
- enumerated_exercises (with icons preserved)

No C-codes or CG labels MUST be consulted, inferred, or pattern-matched
during Pass 1. The CG document is prohibited until Pass 1 is complete.


Rule 2 | Pass 2 — Core CG identification and core competency selection
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The system opens the Curricular Goals document.

2a — Core CG identification.
The single CG whose domain description best matches the chapter's organizing
purpose is selected. Selection is made from CG-level headings and
descriptions only; C-code definitions are NOT opened at this step.

All NINE CGs are eligible as core_cg — both content CGs (CG-1 Number Sense,
CG-2 Algebra, CG-3 Geometry, CG-4 Measurement, CG-5 Data Handling) and
process CGs (CG-6 Reasoning, CG-7 Creativity / Problem Posing, CG-8
Computational Thinking, CG-9 History / Culture). A chapter whose central
organizing purpose is puzzle-solving may legitimately land CG-7 or CG-8 as
core_cg rather than CG-1.

The selected CG is locked as core_cg. No further CG-level comparison is
performed.

2b — Core competency selection (within core_cg only).
Only the C-codes under the locked core_cg are read. The system selects up
to TWO core_competencies whose definitions the chapter summary substantively
addresses.

A second core_competency requires independent justification from a distinct
strand of the chapter — not as a vehicle for or sub-step of the first. If
no second C-code independently qualifies, the system MUST stop at one core
competency.

For each core_competency, the system MUST produce:
- c_code
- justification: one-to-two sentences citing the section(s) or enumerated
  item(s) of the summary that substantively address this competency.


Rule 3 | Pass 3 — Adjunct competency scan (cross-CG)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The system scans C-codes across all CGs OTHER THAN core_cg. The system
selects up to THREE adjunct_competencies whose definitions the chapter
substantively engages but which are not the chapter's central organizing
purpose.

Adjunct selection criterion. The chapter MUST contain at least one named
section, enumerated activity, enumerated worked example, or enumerated
exercise that requires the student to perform the specific cognitive
operation the adjunct competency defines. Shared vocabulary, passing
mention, or incidental appearance is insufficient.

Adjunct competencies are expected to draw most frequently from CG-6, CG-7,
CG-8, or CG-9 in chapters where core_cg is a content CG (1–5). Conversely,
in chapters where core_cg is a process CG (6–9), adjuncts are expected to
draw from content CGs. The scan, however, is not restricted by this
expectation — any non-core CG is eligible.

For each adjunct_competency, the system MUST produce:
- c_code
- justification: one-to-two sentences citing the section(s) or enumerated
  item(s) of the summary that substantively engage this competency.

If fewer than three adjunct competencies qualify under the criterion, the
system MUST stop at the number that qualify. The system MUST NOT pad the
list.


================================================================================
Dissolution test
================================================================================

Rule 4 | Dissolution test
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
After core and adjunct competencies are selected, the system MUST write the
dissolution test sentence in the form:

"This chapter builds the student's ability to [verb] [object] by
[cognitive mechanism]."

The sentence MUST name the operation associated with core_cg and its
core_competencies. Adjunct competencies MUST NOT drive the dissolution
test.

The dissolution test is the single sentence a teacher could remove from
the chapter's purpose to collapse the chapter's organizing intent. It is
the chapter's structural core, stated as an action verb.


================================================================================
Effort index composite
================================================================================

Rule 5 | Effort index composite
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The system reads the four effort signals from the chapter summary JSON:

- conceptual_demand (integer 1 – 3)
- activity_count    (integer)
- demo_count        (integer)
- exec_load         (integer 0 – 2)

These signals are determined at chapter-summary time (not here) and are
copied through unmodified into the mapping output.

The system computes the composite using the Mathematics weights:

    effort_index = (conceptual_demand × 2)
                 + (activity_count    × 1.5)
                 + (demo_count        × 1)
                 + (exec_load         × 2)

Sum of weights = 6.5. This matches the Science sum so that composites are
cross-subject-comparable in the Allocate tab. The Mathematics redistribution
(activity_count weight raised from 1 to 1.5, demo_count weight lowered from
1.5 to 1) reflects the subject's higher reliance on student-executed
practice and its lower reliance on teacher demonstration relative to
Science.

The system MUST NOT re-adjudicate the four signals.


================================================================================
Prohibited documents
================================================================================

Rule 6 | Prohibited documents
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The following documents MUST NOT be consulted at any point during mapping:

- Learning Outcomes         — grade-bound milestones; bypass structural analysis
- Pedagogy documents        — govern instructional execution, not mapping
- Syllabus documents        — introduce curriculum sequence; violate
                              chapter isolation
- Assessment Framework docs — summative evaluation design; structurally
                              distinct
- Position Papers           — philosophical scaffolding; not a mapping
                              instrument

The only external document the mapping procedure consults is the Curricular
Goals document (cg_middle_mathematics.txt or its grade-appropriate
equivalent).

    "stage":          "middle",
    "subject":        "mathematics",
    "grade":          "vii",
    "chapter_number": 5,
    "chapter_title":  "Parallel and Intersecting Lines",

    "core_cg": "CG-3",

    "core_competencies": [
        {
            "c_code":        "C-3.2",
            "justification": "The chapter's central activity develops properties of angles formed by intersecting and parallel lines, exercised through construction and reasoning tasks across §5.2–§5.4."
        },
        {
            "c_code":        "C-3.4",
            "justification": "A dedicated strand of the chapter builds compass-and-straightedge construction of parallel lines and perpendiculars, with graded construction exercises distinct from the angle-property progression."
        }
    ],

    "adjunct_competencies": [
        {
            "c_code":        "C-6.1",
            "justification": "Math Talk items in §5.3 and §5.4 require the student to justify angle equalities through deductive argument, engaging the reasoning competency."
        },
        {
            "c_code":        "C-9.2",
            "justification": "The chapter references Baudhayana's geometric constructions from the Sulba-Sutras, engaging the historical-contributions competency."
        }
    ],

    "dissolution_test": "This chapter builds the student's ability to identify, justify, and construct relationships between parallel and intersecting lines by applying angle properties at their intersections.",

    "conceptual_demand": 2,
    "activity_count":    5,
    "demo_count":        1,
    "exec_load":         1,
    "effort_index":     13.5
}

The example values above illustrate the schema. Actual values per chapter
are determined by Rules 1 – 5 above.
