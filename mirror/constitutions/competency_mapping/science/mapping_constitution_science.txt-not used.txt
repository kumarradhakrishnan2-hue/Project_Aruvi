================================================================================
INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

Each Grade VII Science chapter is studied against the NCF Middle Stage Curricular
Goals document. Call 1 reads the chapter PDF: it builds the chapter summary and
computes four effort index signals that govern period allocation. Call 2 determines
competency: it identifies the CG whose domain matches the chapter, then selects up
to two C-codes within that CG whose definitions the chapter substantively addresses.
A dissolution test sentence records the reasoning. The resulting competency record —
CG, C-codes, dissolution test, co_central flag — is fixed per chapter and held as a
pre-authorised reference for Grade VII.

================================================================================
CALL 1 — CHAPTER PDF: SUMMARY AND EFFORT INDEX
================================================================================

Rule 1 | Evidence base
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The system SHALL read the full chapter PDF. The evidence base comprises four
elements:
  (a) Embedded student activities — boxed tasks labelled "Let us explore /
      investigate / construct / experiment" and equivalents, classified as
      student-executed or teacher-demonstrated.
  (b) End-of-chapter exercises ("Let Us Enhance Our Learning").
  (c) Embedded diagram tasks — questions placed beneath body-text figures
      requiring the student to read or reason from the diagram.
  (d) Body-text thought prompts — italicised questions or "can you think of"
      invitations in the explanatory text.
Explanatory prose with no accompanying student task and informational sidebar
boxes with no student task are excluded.

Rule 2 | Effort index signals
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The system SHALL compute four signal values from the evidence base and pass them
in memory to Call 2.

  conceptual_demand (1–3):
    Score 1 if recall and familiar-context questions dominate the exercise (>60%).
    Score 2 if causal explanation or application to familiar context dominates, or
            demand is evenly spread.
    Score 3 if unfamiliar-context transfer, multi-step causal chain reasoning, or
            investigative design questions constitute 30% or more of the exercise.

  activity_count (integer):
    Count student-executed physical activities only. Exclude teacher demonstrations
    and discussion tasks.

  demo_count (integer):
    Count teacher-performed demonstrations only.

  exec_load (0–2):
    Score 0 if exercise is predominantly written reasoning.
    Score 1 if multi-step calculation or diagram production is 30–60% of questions.
    Score 2 if >60%.

  effort_index = (conceptual_demand × 2) + (activity_count × 1)
               + (demo_count × 1.5) + (exec_load × 2)

effort_index is the sole period allocation input for the Planning Tool.

Rule 3 | Chapter summary
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The system SHALL produce a chapter summary of 600–900 words mirroring the
textbook's section headings in order. The summary describes what the chapter
teaches — its concepts, phenomena, and content organisation. It notes the
existence of activities without elaborating them. It does not describe exercises.
Written to mirror as ch_NN_summary.txt.

================================================================================
CALL 2 — COMPETENCY MAPPING
================================================================================

Rule 4 | Pre-authorised competency list — development method
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The competency assignment for each Grade VII Science chapter was determined by a
two-step process applied to every chapter.

  Step 1 — CG identification:
    The chapter summary is read and the single CG whose domain description matches
    the chapter's subject matter is selected. Selection is made from CG-level
    headings only; C-code definitions are not opened at this step. CG-6 through
    CG-9 are eligible only if no CG-1 through CG-5 domain matches — which applies
    only to orientation or epistemology chapters. The selected CG is locked.

  Step 2 — C-code selection:
    Only the C-codes under the locked CG are read. A maximum of two C-codes are
    selected — those whose definitions the chapter summary substantively addresses.
    A second C-code requires independent justification from a distinct strand of
    the chapter, not as a vehicle for the first.

The dissolution test sentence is then written:
    "This chapter builds the student's ability to [verb] [object]
     by [cognitive mechanism]."

co_central is set to true when two C-codes are selected.

The resulting assignments for all 12 Grade VII chapters are fixed in the
pre-authorised reference table below and are not re-derived at runtime.

Rule 5 | Effort index merge
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The effort index signals computed in Call 1 (conceptual_demand, activity_count,
demo_count, exec_load, effort_index) are merged into the pre-authorised competency
record for the chapter. All competency fields remain unchanged.

Rule 6 | Summary path
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The summary_path field in the output JSON points to the ch_NN_summary.txt file
written in Call 1, Rule 3.
Format: mirror/chapters/science/grade_vii/summaries/ch_NN_summary.txt

Rule 7 | Output write
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The completed JSON record is written to:
  mirror/chapters/science/grade_vii/mappings/ch_NN_mapping.json

The record is also appended to the consolidated file:
  mirror/chapters/science/grade_vii/mappings/chapter_mappings_science_vii.json
